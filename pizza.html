PIZZA MARGHERITA

The margherita pizza was created in 1889 in honor of Queen Margherita of Savoy. At its creation, the pizza contained the three colors of the Italian flag, the red of the tomato sauce, the green of the basil and the white of the mozzarella cheese.

Ingredients

  • 350 gr wheat flour (and a little more for kneading)
  • 180 ml water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon dry yeast
  • 2 ½ teaspoons olive oil
  • 6 tablespoons pomodoro sauce
  • 1 cup buffalo mozzarella cheese
  • 2 small chontos tomatoes, cut into slices
  • ½ cup halved olives
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil leaves
  • Oregano, peperoncino and pepper to serve

Preparation

  1. Mix the flour and salt in a large bowl and separately dissolve the yeast in the water. Make a hole in the center of the flour, pour in the water and start mixing from the center outwards, uniting all the ingredients. Then add the oil and place the dough on a floured work surface; knead for 10 minutes or until smooth and elastic.
  2. Let it rest for an hour in the bowl covered with a cloth or a whole day in the refrigerator (covered with plastic wrap).
  3. Preheat the oven to 450°f. / 232°C. Place a large tin on the bottom rack of the oven or use a pizza stone (if you have one).
  4. Give the dough a sharp punch and place it on the floured work surface. Roll out using a rolling pin and your fingertips. If it sticks to the table, add a little more flour.
  5. When you have a circle of approximately 35 cm, transfer the dough to a floured board.
  6. Spread the surface with the pomodoro sauce but do not get to the edges. Spread the rest of the ingredients, except the basil. Place the pizza on the tin or stone in the oven and bake for 12 to 15 minutes or until the cheese has melted and the pizza is golden brown.
  7. Add the basil and you're done!
